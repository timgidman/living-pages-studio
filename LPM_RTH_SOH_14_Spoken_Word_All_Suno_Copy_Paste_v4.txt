LPM RTH - Songs of Healing
14 Spoken-Word Suno Copy/Paste Packet v4

Format rule: LYRICS first, STYLE second, Song Name listed last inside STYLE.
Voice rule: Male by default; optional Female bracket provided inside STYLE.
Scripture rule: No scriptures spoken or sung for this spoken-word series.
Ending rule: Each song closes with 'I need one safe step forward.' followed by a brief interlude, then 'God is with us for each small step, starting today.' and a 2-second exit sound.

LPM-RTH-SOH-01 - Room to Breathe
RTH Finding Area: Past Pain, Rejection, and Grief
========================================================================================

LYRICS
[Very soft acoustic guitar chord intro, barely heard, about 3 seconds. Begin speaking immediately.]

[Spoken - firm clear male voice, voice forward]
What happened to you matters.
It may still echo in your thoughts,
in your body,
in the way you trust,
and in the way you pull back when life gets too close.

Past pain, rejection, and grief can make a person feel like they have to stay guarded all the time.
But guarded is not the same as healed.
And silence is not the same as peace.

You do not have to explain everything today.
You do not have to fix your whole life today.
The first need is simple:
room to breathe,
room to tell the truth,
and one safe step toward healing.

[Very soft acoustic guitar interlude, barely heard, about 3 seconds. Begin speaking immediately.]

If your heart has been carrying old pain,
you may need a safe person,
a safe place,
and a slower pace.
Not pressure.
Not shame.
Not someone telling you to hurry up and get over it.

Healing begins when the hurt can finally be named without letting it name you.
You are not only what happened.
You are not only who left.
You are not only what broke.
There is more to your story than the pain.

The encouragement for today is this:
start small,
stay honest,
and let help come close enough to matter.
One clear step is still a real step.
One honest sentence is still movement.
One safe conversation can open a door that pain kept closed for years.

[Very soft acoustic guitar interlude, barely heard, about 3 seconds. Begin speaking immediately.]

God can meet you on that road as a guide,
not forcing you,
not rushing you,
but walking near enough to help you take the next step.
Even if trust feels hard,
even if prayer feels distant,
even if you are not sure what you believe today,
you can still begin with this:
I need room to breathe.
I need help that does not bruise.
The next honest sentence can be:
I need one safe step forward.

[Very soft acoustic guitar interlude, barely heard, about 3 seconds. Then final words.]

[Final Spoken Words - calm, steady, reverent]
God is with us for each small step, starting today.

[Soft exit sound - one very quiet acoustic guitar chord fading out, about 2 seconds]

STYLE
Spoken-word only. Do not sing. Use a firm, clear adult male voice speaking with calm authority, warmth, and clarity. [Voice choice: Male / Female — default for this series: Male.] The spoken voice must stay forward and easy to hear at all times. Background music should be extremely quiet and barely heard under the spoken voice. No choir. No organ. No church-service feel. No sung chorus. No long instrumental gaps.
